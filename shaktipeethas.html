Shakti Peetha

Shakti Peetha are spiritual pilgrimage destinations devoted to Goddess Sati. Goddess Sati, an aspect of Goddess Adi Shakti, is known to be extremely fiery, with a frightening temper. However, she is also loving and extremely benevolent by nature. There are total 51 Shakti Peetha, of which

  • Adi Shakti Peetha - 4
  • Maha Shakti Peetha(Asthadasa Shakti Peetha) - 18
  • Shakti Peetha - 29

History of Shakti Peetha

Brahmaputra Prajapati Daksha's daughter Sati devi was an incarnation of Goddess Adi-Shakti. Maa Sati got married to Lord Shiva after years of prayers and meditation. During Agni's yajña, everyone stood up when Prajapati Daksha arrived, except Lord Shiva. Daksha felt insulted by Lord Shiva's behaviour. When Daksha organised a yajña, neither Lord Shiva and Sati were invited. Feeling insulted by this, Lord Shiva refused to attend but Sati went anyway. There, she was not received properly by any of her family members, and her father insulted Lord Shiva. Not being able to tolerate her husband's insult, she feels anger, shame, and regret and sacrifices herself in Agni.

Upon hearing this, Lord Shiva is filled with anger and incarnates Veerbhadra from his jhatha to kill Daksha. Lord Shiva goes to take Sati's body from the Agni and starts Tandav Nritya, terrorising the world. To stop the nritya, Lord Vishnu sends his Sudarshan Chakra to cut Sati's body into pieces. These pieces fell at several places in India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal and Tibet called Shakti Peetha.
